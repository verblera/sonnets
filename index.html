Simple Presentation is quite simple : you will get a very very short biography of the author and a sonnet itself, presented in stanzas.

The Metric Scheme contains some information about the particular type of a sonnet and its metric structure: types of stanzas, rhyme scheme and a number of lines (unsurprisingly, always 14).

If you want to find words to rhyme, you can go to rhymes: you will find a list of rhymed words and their part of speech. Attention: their part of speech is defined in a context. So, if you see a word 'attempt', it is defined only as one type of speech, even though it can be both a verb and a noun.
